## The problem we refused to ignore

Every foil session starts the same way. You unpack the board, lay out the gear, reach for the foil — and then you stop. Four screws. A wrench you may or may not have. Sand already working its way into threads that were designed before anyone thought about corrosion.

We got tired of it. Not in a complaining way. In the way that makes an engineer sit down and refuse to leave the workshop until the problem is solved.

That was three years ago.

## What we actually built

MastLOCK is not a screw replacement. It's a complete rethinking of how a mast connects to a board.

The mechanism is a precision cam-lock system machined from 6061-T6 aluminium — the same grade used in aerospace fasteners. The cam lever operates in a single motion. Pull to release, push to lock. The mast seats in a self-aligning collar that eliminates play in all three axes.

**Setup time: 0.4 seconds.** Not a marketing figure. We timed it with a high-speed camera. Average of 47 trials across four testers, wet hands, dock conditions.

## Why precision matters more than convenience

Most people hear "faster setup" and think convenience. That's not the point.

The point is **repeatability**.

When your mast position is identical every session — same angle, same depth, same tension — your muscle memory transfers. Your tuning carries over. Your sessions build on each other instead of starting from a slightly different baseline each time.

Foil coaches particularly understand this. When you're teaching someone to ride, every variable you eliminate is a lesson you don't have to undo.

## The same mount. Every time. For every board.

MastLOCK uses the standard US base plate pattern. It fits every board with a US box — which is essentially every production foil board made in the last eight years. You don't need to choose between brands. You don't need adapters.

We made it brand agnostic on purpose. The foil industry fragments enough. The mounting system shouldn't be part of that.
